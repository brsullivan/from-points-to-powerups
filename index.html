From Points to Power-Ups:

How Gamification Can Level Up Your User Experience

Speaker

Braelyn Sullivan

Assumptions & Expectations

  • Games are universal
  • Games can look very different
  • Everyone plays games

Throughout this presentation, I will be making a couple of assumptions about games and the people who play them. By definition, these will always be assumptions because it would be impossible to figure out how each of the more than 7 billion people on the planet feel about games; however, I want to set up some expectations so that you'll understand why I feel comfortable making these assumptions.

First, I believe it's safe to assume games are universal because there are examples of games dating back over 5,000 year and spanning across the millenia. We also know games have been found in various cultures all across the world.

Second, I think it is important to understand that games can look very different, even while employing the same strategies and techniques. Sudoku, Clue and Call of Duty aren't likely to have many common characteristics, but each has a set of rules with specific conditions for success.

Finally, because of this wide variety of games and vast history of cultural significanc, I feel confident in assuming everyone enjoys at least one game.

Games come in many shapes & sizes:

  • Video Games (console, mobile, multiplayer, singleplayer, online, casual)
  • Card Games (solitaire, poker)
  • Group party games (Mafia)
  • Brain Teasers (sudoku, crossword, SET)
  • Board Games (Clue, Sorry)

What is Gamification?

Gamification is the process of applying game mechanics to any non-game activity.
